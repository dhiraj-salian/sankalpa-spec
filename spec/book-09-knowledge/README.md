# Book 09 — Knowledge

*Status: **Draft-complete** (all chapters authored) · Nature: Normative. · Reflects: realizes P2, P6, P10.*

## Scope
The knowledge system: durable, curated understanding (not short-term memory) that improves future planning. Human-readable knowledge lives in an **Obsidian-compatible vault**; machine-readable knowledge lives in a **graph database**; the two stay synchronized.

## Chapters
1. **`01-knowledge-vs-memory.md`** *(Normative)* — The distinction and why it matters; Knowledge as first-class, versioned Resources.
2. **`02-knowledge-taxonomy.md`** *(Normative)* — Facts, architecture, projects, policies, documentation, runbooks, playbooks, meetings, people, organizations, capabilities, workflows, experience, relationships.
3. **`03-vault-model.md`** *(Normative)* — The Obsidian-compatible vault: file/link conventions, front-matter, and human-editing workflow.
4. **`04-graph-model.md`** *(Normative)* — The graph schema: node/edge types, identity, and query surface for planners.
5. **`05-vault-graph-synchronization.md`** *(Normative)* — Bidirectional sync semantics, conflict resolution, and consistency guarantees between vault and graph.
6. **`06-knowledge-in-planning.md`** *(Normative)* — How planners retrieve and cite knowledge; provenance and trust levels; how secrets are *never* stored here (P7).
7. **`07-knowledge-packs.md`** *(Normative)* — Distributable Knowledge as Packages (Book 12).
