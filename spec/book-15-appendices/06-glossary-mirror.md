# Book 15 · Chapter 06 — Glossary Mirror

*Nature: **Informative** (a pointer; the Glossary is authoritative).*

> This chapter is a pointer, not a copy. The single source of truth for terminology is the repository-root [Glossary](../../GLOSSARY.md) (Book 00 §Ch04). Duplicating it here would risk drift and violate the single-source-of-truth policy — so this chapter deliberately does not restate definitions; it directs you to the one authoritative place.

## 1. The Glossary is authoritative

Every capitalized proper-noun term in the specification is defined once, in the [Glossary](../../GLOSSARY.md) (Book 00 §Ch04 §1). If you need a term's meaning, that is the place. If a Book's prose and the Glossary appear to differ on a *term*, the Glossary governs the term (and the owning Book governs the full concept, Book 00 §Ch04 §3).

## 2. Why this chapter does not copy it

The terminology policy (Book 00 §Ch04) mandates a *single source of truth*. A mirrored copy would inevitably drift from the original as terms evolve, producing two authorities — exactly the coherence hole the policy forbids. So this appendix, though listed in the Book outline for completeness, is a *link*, not a duplicate. This is itself an example of the anti-duplication discipline the specification applies to everything (one identity, one authority — cf. Book 09 §Ch04 §2, Book 02 §Ch06).

Terminology lives in the [Glossary](../../GLOSSARY.md), authoritatively and once. This chapter points there rather than copying, honoring the single-source-of-truth policy. Use the Glossary for *what a term means* and the companion indexes above for *where a concept is specified*.
