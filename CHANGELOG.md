# Changelog

All notable changes to the Sankalpa specification are recorded here. The format is based on [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/); this repository versions the **specification**, following [Semantic Versioning](https://semver.org/) applied to normative meaning (see [`process/versioning-and-stability.md`](process/versioning-and-stability.md)).

## [Unreleased]

### Added
- **RFC-0012 (`Proposed`) — an interim review process for the bootstrap period** ([`rfcs/0012`](rfcs/0012-interim-review-process.md), [`process/interim-review.md`](process/interim-review.md), [`process/interim-acceptance-ledger.md`](process/interim-acceptance-ledger.md), [`templates/interim-self-review-template.md`](templates/interim-self-review-template.md)). Governance, non-normative to `spec/`. The documented quorum (Domain Lead + ≥2 Reviewers) is unsatisfiable while every role is vacant, and the practice so far waived it ad hoc in prose (RFC-0011 accepted same-day, reviewer gate waived). RFC-0012 replaces the bare waiver with **bootstrap authority** exercised only through a **compensating-control package** — a ≥3-working-day cooling-off, an adversarial self-review, an independent adversarial pass, and a provenance stamp — logs every interim acceptance to a standing **re-review ledger** that must be cleared by seated reviewers before the Phase 2 v1.0 gate, and **auto-lapses per domain** as domains are staffed. Per the founding maintainer's decision, high-stakes changes (security invariants, IR, Kernel API) take the same interim rules as any change, backstopped by the ledger and the pre-v1.0 re-review. Amends `GOVERNANCE.md` (new §7 "Interim governance during bootstrap"; amendment rule renumbered to §8 and marked bootstrap-suspended), `MAINTAINERS.md` (a Bootstrap-authority entry), and notes the substitution in `process/rfc-process.md` §7 and `process/review-gates.md`. New Glossary terms: Interim Review Process, Bootstrap Authority, Compensating Control, Interim-Acceptance Ledger.
  - **Dogfooded:** RFC-0012 is itself the first artifact processed under its own rules — `Proposed`, not Accepted, because its own §4.2 cooling-off has not elapsed. The grandfathered class (RFCs 0001–0011, ADRs 0000–0002, Books 00–15, Phase 0 research) is entered in the ledger as `pending` rather than retrofitted.

### Changed
- **RFC-0011 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes — **including a retraction and a breaking contract change**:
  - **Semantics-preservation enforcement (RFC-0011)**: IR-P10 is a named IR principle and the compiler's transform contract, and Book 05 §02 §4 claimed it was enforced — *"a pass that produces IR failing verification, **or that a semantics-preservation check flags**, is rejected … Trust is never extended to a pass's claim of correctness"*. **No such check existed anywhere in `spec/`.** The checks that did exist (Low IR re-verification, RuntimeGraph conformance) are **predicates on a single artifact**: Book 04 §08 is explicit that verification is *"a total function over IR"* that accepts *"a module"* and checks *"well-formedness"*. The original was never consulted, and equivalence of two arbitrary modules is undecidable — so re-verification was not a weaker form of the missing check but a **different** one. An untrusted third-party pass emitting a well-formed, well-typed, correctly-effect-declared, deterministic, secret-safe module with **different semantics** passed every gate and executed. Security-relevant: a malicious pass needs no undeclared effect, no non-determinism, and no secret — it **retargets a declared, already-approved effect** (rewriting an approved transfer's payee) and isolation, least-privilege, and re-verification are all silent, because the output is *valid*. Determinization's gate-6 backstop cited the phantom check. Adds mandatory **effect-graph conservation** (multiset, order, **target identity**, monotonicity — the first *comparative* check in the pipeline), evaluated **modulo a declared effect-refinement relation**; **dead-effect witnesses** and re-checked analysis facts (following Book 04 §08 §2.6's "turn an undecidable question into a checkable artifact"); and opt-in **translation validation**, **required** for any pass running after an `Approval` is lowered in. **Retracts** *"safe by construction: even a buggy optimization pass cannot corrupt a compilation"* (Book 05 §03) and states the real, finite bound; corrects Book 05 §05 §5 (single-artifact vs. comparative, and names the **backend** gap as a companion finding rather than implying it is closed); bounds Book 11 §10 §4's pass claim. **Breaking**: the pass contract gains witnesses/analysis facts (staged migration per RFC-0011 §13) — the only non-additive change in the batch. Touches Books 02 §07, 04 §02, 05 §02/§03/§05/§06, 11 §10.
  - New Glossary terms: dead-effect witness, effect-graph conservation, equivalence certificate, translation validation.
- **RFC-0010 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes — **including a retraction**:
  - **Runtime observability egress verification (RFC-0010)**: the log secret-freedom guarantee was stated as *hard* and *"enforced, not merely intended"*, grounded in there being *"nothing secret to log on those paths"* and the Observability Manager operating on an *"already-secret-free"* Event stream. **That premise was false at the one place it matters.** The runtime is simultaneously the **sole holder of a plaintext secret** (Book 06 §06) and an **untrusted plugin** (Book 11 §10), and the Event stream is populated **from its output** — `events()` returns `RuntimeEvent`s reflected onto the bus and into the RFC-0002 reasoning ledger. Their secret-freedom rested on an unverified `MUST` addressed to untrusted code plus a **pre-admission** conformance test a malicious runtime passes and then ignores. Meanwhile Book 11 §10 claimed a plugin *"cannot smuggle … a secret … the output is checked at the boundary"* while `RuntimeEvent`s were in no verified-output list, and its bounded-worst-case (*"cannot reach the Secret Broker's values"*) excluded the one plugin class that receives them by design. Adds **observability-egress verification** at the Runtime Manager against a per-Execution **materialized-value digest set** (keyed HMAC under a key the runtime never holds), fail-closed with a `SecretEgressViolation` condition, escalation and attribution, and **no auto-revocation** (containment is already won on the data path; auto-revocation would be a self-inflicted-DoS lever). Adds **SI-13**. **Retracts** the false basis in Book 14 §04 §2 and Book 03 §12 §2–3, and **bounds** Book 11 §10 §4/§6 and §02 §B2/§3 to what is actually true — including stating, normatively, that the check covers verbatim and declared-encoding emission and does **not** bound a transforming or B4-exfiltrating runtime, which effect declaration, egress policy, and isolation govern. Touches Books 02 §04, 03 §12, 06 §02/§03, 11 §02/§10/§11, 14 §04.
  - New Glossary terms: materialized-value digest set, observability egress verification.
- **RFC-0007 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes:
  - **Scheduling admission liveness (RFC-0007)**: the Scheduler was specified to "apply fairness across workspaces/tenants", "honor per-work **deadlines** and **priorities** without **starving** low-priority tenants", and "hold or **shed** work deterministically" — all **asserted with no mechanism, no fields, and no lifecycle outcome**. No schedulable Resource carried a priority or deadline, so the promise named inputs that did not exist; priority without aging or reservation *cannot* deliver starvation-freedom; and `Pending` ("cannot yet begin") had no exit, so an unadmittable `Execution` could sit **indefinitely and invisibly** — the one silent stall in a spec that forbids them everywhere else. Adds **`schedulingClass`** (policy-mapped, capability-gated so priority is governed, not self-asserted) and optional **`deadline`** as `spec` fields; a normative **starvation-freedom floor** (bounded wait via aging and/or *static* per-tenant weighted-fair reservation — learned reservations are excluded because fairness is a guarantee, not a choice among already-equivalent options per Book 10 §05); an explicit **hold vs. shed** split where a held unit always carries a surfaced `AdmissionPending` condition and an unadmittable one reaches `Failed`/**`Shed`**; and a **`DeadlineExceeded`** terminal that compensates and escalates via `CompensationFailed` where compensation itself fails. Disambiguates the two senses of "admission" (store- vs run-), so `Pending` is bounded and never silent. A rescheduled Execution carries its original deadline. Touches Books 02 §04/§07, 03 §07/§13, 14 §02.
  - New Glossary terms: aging, DeadlineExceeded, run-admission, scheduling class, shed (work).
- **RFC-0005 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes:
  - **Secret materialization stability and the secret carve-out (RFC-0005)**: materialization was specified per-instruction and live-from-the-Broker, with nothing guaranteeing that the *same* `SecretRef` materialized twice **within one Execution** yields the same value — so a rotation landing mid-run could silently change a resolved binding (breaking a session token, a multi-part upload, a sign-then-verify pair), leaving the determinism guarantee's "same resolved bindings" clause unmet for any plan that uses one credential twice. Book 11 §04's "consumers are unaffected because they hold references, not values" was true only across *separate* executions. Adds **intra-execution stability** keyed `(SecretRef, grantedBy)`; makes explicit that the memoization caches the **value, never the authority** — every `SecretUse` re-runs the capability and policy checks, so revocation still bites *"even past any caching"* (Book 11 §03 §5), which now has a named cache to speak to. Rotation and revocation are **no longer symmetric**: rotation takes effect at Execution boundaries, revocation immediately. Names the **secret carve-out** to the determinism guarantee (a materialized secret is a resolved binding P7 makes unrecordable) and adds the secret-free **`rotationGeneration`**, letting re-execution replay fail closed on a changed credential rather than silently performing an effect the recorded run never performed; reconstruction replay is unaffected, materializing no secret at all. Touches Books 01 §05, 02 §07, 04 §04, 06 §03/§06, 11 §03/§04.
  - New Glossary terms: intra-execution secret stability, rotation generation, secret carve-out (determinism).
- **RFC-0009 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes:
  - **Channel identity binding and assurance (RFC-0009)**: a channel's transport identity was *"mapped to a Session/User"* — a **mapping, never an authentication** — while the same chapters declare the channel **untrusted**. Front-door auth (tokens/mTLS/OIDC) does not reach a Telegram message or an email `From`, so the Kernel took an untrusted adapter at its word about *who is speaking*: a spoofed or compromised handle could issue Intents and continue Conversations as the user, and cross-channel continuity ("starts in Slack, continues by email") silently extended a Session's authority to an unauthenticated leg. Adds the **`ChannelBinding`** core kind (verified enrollment from an already-authenticated context; workspace-scoped, revocable, audited), a fixed totally-ordered **assurance level** (`low`<`medium`<`high`) that **caps a Session's effective authority**, **step-up** for consequential actions to the Web Runtime (which already hosts approval, so the approval page *is* the step-up), and cross-channel continuity that **re-binds rather than inherits**. Attribution now records assurance, so audit answers not just *who* but *how strongly proven*. Touches Books 02 §07, 11 §02/§07/§08, 13 §02/§03/§06.
  - New Glossary terms: assurance level, channel binding, enrollment, step-up.
- **RFC-0008 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes:
  - **Grant re-authorization on upgrade (RFC-0008)**: capability grants were established once, at install, and the upgrade flow had **no re-authorization step** — so an upgrade that kept the same declared capabilities but shipped different code (a compromised key, or a package-ownership takeover) silently inherited `payments`, secret-materialization, and egress grants for code no one reviewed. Grants now record an **authorized-against identity** (version + publisher + signing key), and upgrade re-evaluates them: broadened authority is surfaced for explicit authorization; a **signing-identity change re-authorizes sensitive-class capabilities even when the capability set is unchanged**; same-identity non-broadening upgrades may carry forward frictionlessly; narrowed-away grants are auto-revoked. Rollback to a previously-authorized version needs no new consent, but a target *wider than the current grant set* is treated as an upgrade — closing the ship-narrow-then-roll-back evasion. Book 12 §04's "a compromised publisher cannot escalate beyond the capabilities a workspace explicitly grants" is tightened: true for *net-new* authority, it did not cover **re-use of granted authority by substituted code**. Touches Books 11 §03/§06, 12 §04/§05.
  - New Glossary terms: authorized-against identity, grant carry-forward, upgrade re-authorization.
- **RFC-0006 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening batch 2). Normative changes:
  - **Base-version three-way Knowledge reconcile (RFC-0006)**: vault⇄graph sync no longer rests on optimistic concurrency alone, which a **direct/offline vault edit cannot satisfy** — such an edit observes no `resourceVersion`, so compare-and-swap had nothing to check and the "no lost update" guarantee was unbacked for the vault's primary edit path. Adds the controller-managed **`syncedFrom`** merge-base stamp to note front-matter and a three-way reconcile (fast-forward / merge / conflict) keyed on `generation`, with a fail-safe that an absent, malformed, or unknown base is conflict-surfaced and never merged. Trust now adjudicates only where the sides differ in trust; same-trust conflicts are decided by the base. Conflicted Knowledge is **withheld from planning retrieval** (indeterminate, not low-trust) rather than down-weighted. Touches Books 09 §03/§05/§06.
  - New Glossary terms: synced-base stamp, three-way Knowledge reconcile.
- **RFCs 0002–0004 reflected into `spec/` and marked `Final`** (Phase 2 hardening set). Normative changes:
  - **Recorded-reasoning carve-out (RFC-0002)**: the determinism guarantee now explicitly includes recorded `CapturedReasoning`/`Time`/`Random` outputs; introduces the secret-free, content-addressed **reasoning ledger** on `Execution` and two replay modes — **reconstruction** (no effects; defines conformance/golden-file tests) and **re-execution** (effects re-fire under live policy). Touches Books 01 §05, 04 §04/§07, 06 §03, 10 §03.
  - **Shadow sampling for drift (RFC-0003)**: replaces the inert "diverging from fresh reasoning" language with a policy-governed, non-blocking, version-bucketed shadow-sampling mechanism; adds `shadowSource` to substituted invocations. Touches Books 10 §06, 05 §06, 04 §04, 01 §05.
  - **Compensation-failure condition (RFC-0004)**: adds the `CompensationFailed` terminal *condition* (not a phase), best-effort continuation, one-level cascade bound, mandatory escalation to a new **`RemediationTask`** core kind (two-party ack for high-consequence residuals), a failure-taxonomy row, and reconciles Book 10's terminal-state vocabulary. Touches Books 06 §03, 03 §13, 02 §04/§07.
  - New Glossary terms: reasoning ledger, fresh/replay-with-record/reconstruction/re-execution; drift, shadow sampling/source, version bucket, re-validation window; compensation failure, remediation task, residual inconsistency.

### Added
- **Phase 0 research completed — 17 studies authored, all 18 now `Accepted`; the Phase 0 gate is closed** ([`research/`](research/README.md), [`ROADMAP.md`](ROADMAP.md)). Non-normative: no `spec/` text changes, no new Glossary terms.
  - **Prior art (10 new, joining the Kubernetes exemplar)**: [Linux](research/prior-art/linux.md) (syscall stability as the model for our plugin contract; the honest monolith counter-argument to ADR-0002), [LLVM](research/prior-art/llvm.md) (IR-in-the-middle, pass framework, and why we refuse undefined behavior), [MLIR](research/prior-art/mlir.md) (progressive lowering; why we fix exactly two levels rather than open dialects), [Temporal](research/prior-art/temporal.md) (durable execution; determinism by *representation* rather than author discipline), [Git](research/prior-art/git.md) (content addressing; secrets-in-history as the anti-pattern P7 forecloses), [Terraform](research/prior-art/terraform.md) (plan/apply, providers; the state file as a P7 cautionary tale), [Docker](research/prior-art/docker.md) (distribution ergonomics; install ≠ privilege), [Cloudflare Workers](research/prior-art/cloudflare-workers.md) (bindings as the production proof that P8 ships), [PostgreSQL](research/prior-art/postgresql.md) (evidence-driven planning as determinization's closest ancestor; `EXPLAIN` as an obligation), [Rust / Cargo](research/prior-art/rust-cargo.md) (the RFC process; editions; `build.rs` as the ambient-authority failure RFC-0008 answers).
  - **Patterns (7 new)**: [compiler design](research/patterns/compiler-design.md), [DDD](research/patterns/domain-driven-design.md), [CQRS](research/patterns/cqrs.md), [hexagonal architecture](research/patterns/hexagonal-architecture.md), [microkernel](research/patterns/microkernel-architecture.md), [event sourcing](research/patterns/event-sourcing.md), [capability-based security](research/patterns/capability-based-security.md).
  - **Three studies dissent from the design rather than ratify it**, and are the ones worth reading first: the microkernel study states that an 18-Manager core adopts the pattern's *boundary* discipline but not its *size* discipline, forfeiting the assurance minimality buys; the compiler-design study argues determinization is a semantics-**changing** transform justified by evidence — which a classical compiler would call a miscompilation — and asks whether IR-P10 and Book 05 §06 are stated precisely enough to be distinguishable; the CQRS study concludes the pattern is largely *not* load-bearing (reconciliation does the work) and notes Book 01 §06 does not list it as an influence.
  - **Provenance recorded, not elided** ([`research/README.md`](research/README.md#provenance-and-its-limits)): the studies were written **after** Phases 1–2 and accepted **without independent review** (all Domain Leads vacant, Steering Council unseated). The set is a map of the design's intellectual debts, not evidence the design is correct — a study written to explain a decision is a weaker instrument than one written to inform it. `ROADMAP.md` marks Phase 0 "complete, out of order" rather than closing it silently.
  - Satisfies the research action item in [RFC-0011 §Reflection](rfcs/0011-semantics-preservation-enforcement-for-compiler-passes.md) ("add the LLVM/Alive translation-validation study to `research/prior-art/`"): the LLVM study covers translation validation and cites Alive2.
